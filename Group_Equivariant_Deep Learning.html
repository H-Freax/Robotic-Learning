A group (G,⋅) (G, \cdot) (G,⋅)﻿is a set of elements GG G﻿ equipped with a group product ⋅\cdot⋅﻿, a binary operator, that satisfies the following four axioms:

  • Closure: Given two elements g gg﻿ and hhh﻿ of GGG﻿, the product g⋅h g \cdot h g⋅h﻿ is also in GGG﻿.
  • Associativity: Forg,h,i∈Gg, h, i \in G g,h,i∈G﻿ the product ⋅ \cdot⋅﻿ is associative, i.e., g⋅(h⋅i)=(g⋅h)⋅ig \cdot (h \cdot i) = (g \cdot h) \cdot i g⋅(h⋅i)=(g⋅h)⋅i﻿.
  • Identity element: There exists an identity element e∈Ge \in G e∈G﻿ such that e⋅g=g⋅e=ge \cdot g = g \cdot e = g e⋅g=g⋅e=g﻿ for any g∈Gg \in Gg∈G﻿
  • Inverse element: For each g∈Gg \in Gg∈G﻿ there exists an inverse element g−1∈Gg^{-1} \in Gg−1∈G﻿ s.t. g−1⋅g=g⋅g−1=eg^{-1} \cdot g = g \cdot g^{-1} = eg−1⋅g=g⋅g−1=e﻿.
